# Preface

Welcome to the Berkeley Physics 221A Quantum Mechanics course notes.

These notes cover the graduate-level quantum mechanics sequence at UC Berkeley. The notes were originally written in Plain TeX by Professor Robert Littlejohn and have been converted to MyST Markdown for web publishing.

## About This Course

Physics 221A is the first part of the graduate quantum mechanics sequence (221A/221B) at UC Berkeley. The course covers:

- Mathematical formalism of quantum mechanics (Hilbert spaces, operators, bras and kets)
- Postulates of quantum mechanics
- Density operator and mixed states
- Spatial degrees of freedom
- Time evolution
- Approximation methods (perturbation theory, variational method)
- Identical particles
- Scattering theory
- Relativistic quantum mechanics (Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations)

## About These Notes

These lecture notes were developed over many years of teaching Physics 221A/B at UC Berkeley. They are designed to be self-contained and include:

- Detailed derivations and proofs
- Numerous examples and applications
- Exercises and problems at the end of each chapter
- Cross-references to related topics
- Appendices covering mathematical background

## How to Use These Notes

Each chapter is self-contained and covers a specific topic in quantum mechanics. The notes include:

1. **Derivations**: Step-by-step mathematical derivations
2. **Examples**: Worked-out examples illustrating key concepts
3. **Problems**: Exercises at the end of each chapter
4. **Cross-references**: Links to related topics in other chapters
5. **Appendices**: Mathematical background material
